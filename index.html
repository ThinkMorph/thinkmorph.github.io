Abstract

Multimodal reasoning requires iterative coordination between language and vision, yet defining meaningful interleaved chain-of-thought remains unresolved. We posit that text and image thoughts should function as complementaryâ€”rather than isomorphicâ€”modalities that mutually advance reasoning. Guided by this principle, we build ThinkMorph, a unified model fine-tuned on ~24K high-quality interleaved reasoning traces spanning tasks with varying visual engagement. ThinkMorph learns to generate progressive textâ€“image reasoning steps that concretely manipulate visual content while maintaining coherent verbal logic. It delivers large gains on vision-centric benchmarks (average +34.7% over the base model) and generalizes across out-of-domain tasks, matching or surpassing larger and proprietary VLMs. Beyond performance, ThinkMorph exhibits emergent multimodal intelligenceâ€”unseen visual manipulation skills, adaptive switching between reasoning modes, and better test-time scaling through diversified multimodal thoughts.
